Historical Facts About Handball

The beginning

  • The game of handball dates as far back as the Roman Empire
  • The first signs of the game being played in the British Isles were around the 16th century. Finally, the modern game of handball started forming in Ireland in 1850.
  • The game as we know it today was established in Denmark in 1898.
  • The first written set of team handball rules was published in 1906 by the Danish gym teacher, Olympic medalist Holger Nielsen .
  • The official rules of handball were written down in Berlin on October 29th, 1917, by Karl Schelenz .
  • The first official game of handball took place in Germany the same year.
  • Handball was introduced into the Olympics in 1972 for men and 1976 for women.

Handball in Romania

Handball was first introduced in Romania in 1921, when the first game was played at the Central Stadium Sibiu. The game was organised by Professor Wilhelm Binder, and the teams were two local high schools: Brukenthal High School and Girls High School. In 1931, Sibiu organised the first major competition, Transylvania Cup.

According to the Encyclopedia of Handball, Romania has a rich handball history and has produced many talented players over the years. The country has also hosted several major international handball tournaments, including the European Men's Handball Championship in 1962 and the Women's Handball World Championship in 2015 .

Romania was a dominant force in handball for many decades, with four gold medals at the World Championships. However, since 1990, Romania has failed to win a medal.

The Romanian Handball Federation, the governing body for handball in Romania, was established in 1936 and has since been responsible for organising and promoting the sport in the country.
